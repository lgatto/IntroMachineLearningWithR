6.2 Model performance

When investigating multi-class problems, it is good to consider additional performance metrics and to inspect the confusion matrices in more details, to look if some classes suffer from greater mis-classification rates.

Models accuracy can also evaluated using the F1 score, where \(F1 = 2 ~ \frac{precision \times recall}{precision + recall}\), calculated as the harmonic mean of the precision (\(precision = \frac{tp}{tp+fp}\), a measure of exactness – returned output is a relevant result) and recall (\(recall=\frac{tp}{tp+fn}\), a measure of completeness – indicating how much was missed from the output). What we are aiming for are high generalisation accuracy, i.e high \(F1\), indicating that the marker proteins in the test data set are consistently and correctly assigned by the algorithms.

For a multi-class problem, the macro F1 (mean of class F1s) can be used.
